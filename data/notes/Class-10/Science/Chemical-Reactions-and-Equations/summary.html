üìë 5 Main Types of Reactions

  • Combination: Two or more substances combine to form a single product (e.g., Burning Coal)[cite: 98, 109].
  • Decomposition: A single reactant breaks down into simpler products[cite: 122, 123].
    • Thermal: Using heat (e.g., Limestone to Quick Lime)[cite: 128, 129].
    • Electrolysis: Using electricity (e.g., Water)[cite: 132, 156].
  • Displacement: A more reactive element takes the place of a less reactive one[cite: 175, 182].
  • Double Displacement: An exchange of ions between reactants, often forming a precipitate[cite: 194, 196].
  • Redox: Simultaneous Oxidation (gaining oxygen) and Reduction (losing oxygen)[cite: 207, 208, 211].

Mass can neither be created nor destroyed[cite: 45, 46]. Total mass of products must equal total mass of reactants[cite: 47]. We use the Hit-and-Trial method to ensure the number of atoms matches on both sides[cite: 71].
