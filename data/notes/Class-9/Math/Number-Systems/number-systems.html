Number Systems — Quick Notes

Key Concepts

  • Natural numbers: 1, 2, 3… Whole numbers: 0,1,2… Integers: ..., -2,-1,0,1…
  • Rational numbers: numbers expressible as p/q (q≠0). Decimal: terminating or repeating.
  • Irrational numbers: non-terminating, non-repeating decimals (e.g., √2, π).
  • Real numbers: rationals ∪ irrationals. Representation on number line.

Important Results

  • If a is rational ≠0 and b is irrational, then a+b and a×b are irrational.
  • √2 is irrational (proof by contradiction).
  • Between any two rationals there are infinitely many rationals.

Formula Box

  • Convert repeating decimal to fraction: e.g., 0.333... = 1/3
  • Division of fractions: a/b ÷ c/d = ad/bc

Quick Examples / Tips

  • Express 0.272727... as a fraction: 27/99 = 3/11.
  • Find a rational between 3/4 and 4/5: (3/4+4/5)/2 = 31/40.
